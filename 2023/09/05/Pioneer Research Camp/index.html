What I did and learnt in my Pioneer Research Camp

Author: Ivan Li Date: September 5, 2023  0:00:00

I was so honored to get enrolled into the Pioneer research camp, as I’ve long heard that it’s one of the most rigorous online research camp for high school students. I am also worried that the camp being online might pose some trouble when I want to reach out for tangible resources. Luckily, my concentration was computer science, something that requires minimal amount of physical apparatus.

Due to the weird schedulling of our school’s big exam, I had to miss half of each of the first two group sessions. Luckily the professor only talks about some theories regarding computer security, and I easily caught up at home watching the replay. The only regret is I lost the chance to leave a good impression on the professor.

As time went on, we were told to formulate individual research questions, which is always the toughest decision to make. Adding to my limited knowledge regarding computer science in particular, I was really nervous, fearing that I might not be able to finish the program.

Yet a sudden thought struck me. I’ve always been into language models and have practised the implementations in my spare time. What about finding the intersection between large language models and computer security.

Then a topic came to my mind – adversarial machine learning.

Basically how does machine learning fail in its numerous procedures, and how to mitigate the harm it brings.

I was thrilled.

After reading tons of literature and writing code, I finally took a general grasp of this field.

And I was ready for some further research!

The two weeks when I had no idea of what to do really haunted me like a dream. But now looking back on, the experience I gained from the experience was priceless!
